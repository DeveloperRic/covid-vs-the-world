Help stop covid-19

All information sourced from WHO



What everyone can do:

  • Wash your hands frequently

  • Try not to touch your face

  • Distance yourself from others

    If you must go out, keep at least 1 metre/3 feet away form others

  • If you're showing symptoms (even if they're light) seek medical help

    Symptoms include: fever, cough and difficulty breathing

    If you're heading to a clinic/hospital, call in first

  • Follow the advice of your health workers and government officials

If you've returned from a vulnerable area:

In addition to the above...

  • Stay home and avoid contact with others

    Perform self-isolation for 14 days if your government advises you to do so and especially if you're showing symptoms (even if they're light).


What parents can do:

  • Spend one-on-one time with each of your children

    Depending on their age and interest, you can cater what you do with them and for how long.

    Quality time with family goes a long way

  • Create a flexible daily routine

    It's easy for our mental state to deteriorate if we're idle and this is especially true in children.

    Discuss with your children what you'd like to add to your routines.

  • Use positive language

    Communicate what you'd like your child to do, not what you'd like them not to do.

  • Be direct and realistic with consequences for bad behaviour
