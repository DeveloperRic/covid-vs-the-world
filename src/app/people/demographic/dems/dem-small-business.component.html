Article Author: Sebastian Gadzinski

March 29, 2020


Unless your business is making ventilators, masks, or toilet paper, trying to start or run your own business during times like these are extremely tough. Ever since COVID-19 was discovered, large companies all over the world have been experienced drops in sales over time, leading to laying off workers, or even closing the business. “In my one job I was temporarily laid off, and my other one didn’t even message us, they just ghosted us”, Liz Tuomi from Ottawa Ontario. People are afraid to go out and buy things because they don’t want to catch the virus. If these large companies are struggling, imagine the small businesses.

Government Help

Governments are trying to help the small businesses by providing them instant cash, ranges differ between governments. In Canada they are trying to support businesses with 2000$ a month who are not receiving a paycheck as a result of COVID-19, and for the next couple of months, up to a maximum of $1,375 per employee and $25,000 per employer. Along with that the Canada Revenue Agency will defer tax payments until August 31, 2020, to help businesses with cash flow.

Small Businesses That Were Affected the Most

If you’re a restaurant, gym, or coffee shop owner, you have probably closed shop and will stay closed until this virus goes away. These three categories of small businesses everywhere have taken a big hit in the business world due to the restrictions on gathering of people.
